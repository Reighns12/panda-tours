Beneath The Surface

Geothermal & Geological Wonders

The Rift Valley around Nakuru and Naivasha sits on some of the most active geothermal ground in East Africa — here's where to feel it for yourself.

Olkaria Geothermal Spa
Soak in naturally heated pools fed by the same underground forces that power the region's geothermal plants — a relaxing way to unwind after a day on the trails.
Olkaria Geothermal Fields
Vast open fields where steam rises straight from the earth, surrounded by power infrastructure and wild landscapes — a striking look at Kenya's renewable energy story.
Steam Vents of Hell's Gate
Scattered along the gorge walls, natural steam vents hiss from cracks in the rock, a dramatic reminder of the volcanic activity that shaped this entire landscape.
Mount Longonot Volcano
An extinct stratovolcano whose crater rim trail offers a front-row seat to the forces that built — and still shape — the floor of the Great Rift Valley.
Menengai Volcano
One of the world's largest calderas, Menengai is both a geological giant and a source of geothermal energy, with sweeping views into its vast, mist-filled crater.
